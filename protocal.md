# Scratch Assay Protocol — Light Damage Experiment (3T3 Cells)

## Materials
- 3T3 cells
- Multi-well plate (rows A–E, columns 1–12; active wells A5–H12)
- UV light source
- Red light source
- Aluminum foil (to block light on control wells)
- Sterile pipette tips (for scratching)
- Microscope (phase contrast / transmission)

---

## Plate Layout

| Column | Condition | Time |
|--------|-----------|------|
| 1 (rows B–E) | No-light control | paired to each time group |
| 3 | Red light | 10 s |
| 4 | Red light | 1 min |
| 5 | Red light | 5 min |
| 6 | Red light | 10 min |
| 9 | UV light | 10 s |
| 10 | UV light | 1 min |
| 11 | UV light | 5 min |
| 12 | UV light | 10 min |

Rows A–E serve as 5 replicates for each condition.

---

## Protocol

### 1. Cell Preparation
- Seed 3T3 cells in the multi-well plate and incubate until they form a mostly confluent monolayer.
- Check cell health under the microscope: cells should be alive, attached, and evenly distributed.

### 2. Scratch
- Drag a sterile pipette tip straight across the cell layer in each well to create a uniform scratch.
- Take baseline (t = 0) images of every well immediately after scratching (phase contrast).

### 3. Light Exposure
- Cover control wells (column 1) with foil throughout the experiment.
- Expose treatment wells to their assigned light condition (UV or Red) for the assigned duration:
  - 10 seconds
  - 1 minute
  - 5 minutes
  - 10 minutes
- Keep lamp distance and settings identical for all wells.
- At each time point, remove the wells reaching that duration, cover them with foil, and return the remaining wells under the light for the next interval.

### 4. Imaging (Immediately After Exposure)
- Image each well immediately after its light exposure ends (phase contrast / transmission).
- Use the same microscope settings and zoom for all images.
- Imaging order used in this experiment: Red-light wells (cols 3→6), then UV wells (cols 9→12), with matched controls (col 1) interspersed.

### 5. Recovery & 24-Hour Follow-Up
- Return the plate to the incubator.
- Image all wells again at 24 hours (and optionally at 3 days) to assess scratch closure, cell morphology, and survival.

---

## Analysis
Compare across conditions:
- **Scratch closure**: how much the gap has closed at 24 h vs. t = 0
- **Cell morphology**: elongated/spread (healthy) vs. rounded/detached (damaged)
- **Cell density**: debris and cell loss as indicators of light damage

## Expected Results
| Condition | Expected outcome |
|-----------|-----------------|
| UV light | More cell damage; slower or absent wound closure |
| Red light | Less damage; wound closure similar to control |
| No-light control | Normal wound closure baseline |
